# jimmy_aviation
A web gallery for my aviphoto collection

## About this project
I started to become extremely enthusiastic to plane spotting since 2025, but that's also when I felt extremely hard to show off my hobby.

Pictures are all left in the storage card and I got no interests in refining them and posting them, except from instagram, to somewhere I can really be appreciated by those professional photographers, tho I know I'm far away from the standard for that. But my intuition told me at least I gotta give it a try.

I then reached out to JetPhotos, which is probably the biggest aviation photo database around the globe imo. But I got cooked the first time I used it, when I submitted five uploads but all got rejected for shitty reasons.

I tried to break through the most strict standards in the following months, things are getting better but I still only got 2 out of a huge number that I can't remember of photos accepted and screened on their website.

I then come up with an idea of self host a site to display all of my pics, where there'll be no standards, no matter how shitty hte quality is, I can put it there as long as I want. That's the initial inspiration of this project. Also it was affected by my friends who already have nice-looking personal sites.

I didn't mean to copy and host a database like jetphoto does, this would be purely a gallery for self-satisfaction, sounds a bit weird but it is.

## A special feature

One of the factors helps with the planning of a spotting is what's new getting inbound, that is to say, if there's no new liveries or special aircrafts coming in, probably just stay at home.

It can be a hugh pain to scroll the list of arrivals and departures on FR24, which is probably the main tool used for spotters I reckon, as they don't provide much useful filters to quickly retieve what would be attractive. So a prediction feature was added to provide with a filtered list of flights arriving/departuring from the desired ariport as a reference for the planning.
